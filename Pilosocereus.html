Pilosocereus Magnificus

a.k.a. Blue Candle

This is a variable species, but the photograph is a good representation of what they should look like. Even the number of ribs can vary, but usually there are five to seven. The plant branches, both from the base and also higher up the stems. The body is a strong slate blue with whitish areoles that are covered in brownish yellow spines running continuously up the ribs of the plant.

There are normally 10-14 radial spines and six to eight central which are slightly longer than the radials. They are so close together that they look like a continuous dense brown line. When the plant is old enough it will produce a pseudocephalium. Plants can easily get to 6-10ft. tall.
